Selected Experience

üë®üèæ‚Äçüî¨ Professional Experience

Currently, I am working as a Visiting Research Scholar, where I focus on the design, fabrication, and characterization of advanced composite materials. My primary interest lies in developing novel Hybrid Metal Matrix Composites (HMMCs) and optimizing their mechanical and microstructural properties through advanced manufacturing techniques like additive manufacturing and friction stir processing.

Before this, I gained extensive industrial experience as a Junior Engineer and Quality Control Engineer. In these roles, I led root cause analysis for non-conforming materials, developed and optimized CMM inspection programs, and applied Design of Experiments (DOE) to enhance manufacturing processes, ensuring compliance with rigorous AS9100 and ISO 9001 standards.

Additionally, my work as a Research Assistant at the Center for Research in Metallurgy involved the practical application of casting techniques and material characterization. There I focused on analyzing mechanical properties and preparing specimens, which built the foundational knowledge for my current research in material science.
